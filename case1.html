📋 Background

Where it started

When I joined resOS in May 2023, paid campaigns were running but without a clear framework. Budget was split reactively, conversion tracking was broken, and cost-per-lead sat at an average of €50 with no visibility into which channels were driving quality leads.

My first action was an audit: rebuilding GA4 tracking from scratch, unifying UTM parameters across all channels, and creating a single reporting view where channel performance could be directly compared.

🔄 The approach

Systematic, iterative optimisation

Rather than overhauling everything at once, I followed a prioritise → test → scale methodology: identify the highest-leverage lever, run a structured experiment, measure, then kill or scale.

Small wins in Q3 2023 stacked into significant CPL improvements by Q1 2024 — creating budget headroom reinvested into volume.

💡 Key learnings

What two years of iteration taught me

01

Attribution first, always

You can't optimise what you can't measure. Rebuilding tracking was the single highest-ROI action in month one — everything else depended on it being right.

02

Audience consolidation beats fragmentation

More ad sets ≠ more control. Fewer, broader audiences consistently lowered CPMs and gave platform algorithms more signal to optimise against.

03

Creative is the most underestimated lever

Bid strategy and audience optimisation have a ceiling. Creative — especially video and interactive formats — was the most consistent source of step-change CPL improvement.

04

Markets behave differently — use it

What worked in Denmark didn't work in Poland. Localising the mechanic, not just the language, consistently outperformed translated copies of successful campaigns.
